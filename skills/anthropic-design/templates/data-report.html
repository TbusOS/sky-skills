Q1 2026 Report: AI adoption trends across the workforce

Published April 12, 2026 · Authors: Sasha Volkov, Diego Ramirez, Hannah Liu · Anthropic Economic Index team

Drawing on 2.4 million anonymized Claude.ai conversations and a panel survey of 4,800 workers across 14 countries, this report tracks how generative AI is being adopted at work — what tasks people delegate, which industries lead, and where the capability frontier is moving fastest.

1. Adoption by occupation

Software development, marketing, and customer support remain the heaviest users of Claude. The fastest growth this quarter, however, is in legal research and life-sciences — both up more than 60% quarter over quarter as longer context windows make full-document review practical.

2. Augmentation vs. automation

72% of work-related Claude conversations are augmentative — the user remains in the loop and edits the output. Only 28% are automation patterns where the user accepts the response without modification. The augmentation share has held steady for four straight quarters, even as model capability has grown.

[SVG donut chart: augmentation 72% vs automation 28%]

3. Task complexity

Median tokens per Claude conversation rose from 2,100 in Q4 2025 to 4,800 in Q1 2026. The shift reflects two trends: longer source documents being attached, and multi-step tool-use sessions where Claude reads code, edits files, and runs tests inside a single conversation.

[SVG line chart: median tokens per conversation, Q1 2024 – Q1 2026]

4. Geographic spread

The United States, India, and Brazil account for the largest absolute usage. Per-capita adoption is now highest in Estonia, Singapore, and the Netherlands — all three countries where ministries have published official AI productivity guidance for the public sector.

Methodology

All conversation data is anonymized and clustered using a privacy-preserving topic model. Survey responses come from a stratified panel weighted to match each country's labor force composition. Full methodology and replication code are in our methodology appendix.
